Exposure Assessment

Exposure assessment is the process of estimating or measuring the magnitude, frequency and duration of exposure to an agent, along with the number and characteristics of the population exposed. Exposure analysis is the science that describes how an individual or population comes in contact with a contaminant, including quantification of the amount of contact across space and time.Risk is a function of exposure and hazard.

Basically, an exposure assessment implies the counting or estimating of people, property, systems, or other elements present in hazard zones. In this case, for example, even for an extremely high (high hazard) landslide susceptibility, the risk of an adverse outcome is unlikely if exposures are near zero. Conversely, a moderate landslide susceptibility may presents substantial risk if an individual or a population is highly exposed.

Population assessment

The first exposure assessment that has been conducted was the mandatory one; a spatial raster dataset containing the estimated total number of people per gridcell was given:

  1. The raster projection was the WGS84, so it has been reprojected and clipped accordingly;
  2. The susceptibility map has been reclassified in four classes:

    • Low
    • Moderate
    • High
    • Very high
  3. Then, since the resolution of the zones layer should be equal or lower than the input layer, it was also resampled using extension and resolution of the clipped population raster dataset: ~ 81,67 m;
  4. A particular zonal statistic was used in order to derive the population counts in each susceptibility class;

Here the resulting plot shows the percentage of people per each susceptibility class. As you can observe from a critical point of view, the areas with high and very high risk of landslide host almost no people, so it's safe to say that the exposure is not particularly alarming in this case. On the other end, almost half of the inhabitants live nearby moderately risky areas, that have to be monitored for that specific reason.

This information is generated with evidence-based analytics and knowledge using new spatial data mining technologies. The GHSL processing framework uses heterogeneous data including global archives of fine-scale satellite imagery, census data, and volunteered geographic information. The data is processed fully automatically and generates analytics and knowledge reporting objectively and systematically about the presence of population and built-up infrastructures.
